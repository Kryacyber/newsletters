Cybersecurity News Digest: September 2025

Welcome to this week's edition of the Cybersecurity News Digest, where we cover critical disruptions in aviation, emerging vulnerabilities, and high-profile hacker arrests. From zero-click exploits to North Korean-linked cyberattacks, this edition highlights the evolving threats facing organizations today. Stay informed and proactive to safeguard your systems.

General Security News

  • 2025-09-20 Cyberattack Disrupts Check-In Systems at Major European Airports

    A recent cyberattack disrupted electronic check-in systems at major European airports, forcing passengers to rely on manual check-in and boarding processes. The incident underscores the growing risks to critical infrastructure in the aviation sector.

  • 2025-09-19 Summer 2025 SOC 1 Report Now Available

    AWS has released its Summer 2025 SOC 1 report, covering 183 services and demonstrating continuous compliance with security controls. This report provides assurance to customers relying on AWS for secure operations.

  • 2025-09-19 AWS Organizations Now Supports Full IAM Language

    AWS Organizations now supports full IAM language for service control policies, enabling more granular access control and enhanced security for cloud environments.

Policy & Legal

  • 2025-09-19 U.K. Arrests Two Teen Scattered Spider Hackers

    UK authorities have arrested two teenagers linked to the Scattered Spider hacking group, suspected of participating in a 2024 cyberattack against Transport for London. This case highlights the growing threat of youth-driven cybercrime.

Data Breaches

  • 2025-09-20 ShadowLeak Zero-Click Flaw Exploits OpenAI's Deep Research Agent

    Researchers discovered a zero-click exploit in OpenAI's Deep Research agent that could leak Gmail data without user interaction. This vulnerability, codenamed ShadowLeak, was addressed by OpenAI in August.

  • 2025-09-18 SonicWall Warns of Cloud Backup Breach

    SonicWall has detected a breach in its cloud backup service, affecting less than 5% of customers. The company recommends password resets to mitigate potential risks.

Malware

  • 2025-09-21 DPRK Hackers Use ClickFix Lures to Deliver BeaverTail Malware

    North Korean-linked threat actors are using ClickFix-style lures to deliver BeaverTail malware in cryptocurrency and retail sector job scams. This tactic targets marketing and trading roles rather than software development.

  • 2025-09-20 LastPass Warns of Fake macOS Infostealer Campaign

    LastPass has issued a warning about a widespread infostealer campaign targeting macOS users via fake GitHub repositories. The campaign delivers Atomic infostealer malware disguised as legitimate tools.

Vulnerabilities

  • 2025-09-22 Fortra Patches Critical GoAnywhere MFT Vulnerability

    Fortra has patched a critical deserialization vulnerability in GoAnywhere MFT, which could allow command injection attacks. This flaw, tracked as CVE-2025-10035, poses a significant risk to organizations using the software.

  • 2025-09-22 Microsoft Patches Critical Entra ID Flaw

    Microsoft has patched a critical token validation flaw in Entra ID, which could allow attackers to impersonate any user, including Global Administrators. This vulnerability, CVE-2025-55241, has been assigned a CVSS score of 10.0.

Conclusion

This week's cybersecurity news highlights the persistent and evolving nature of cyber threats, from critical infrastructure disruptions to high-profile vulnerabilities and state-sponsored attacks. Organizations must stay vigilant, apply patches promptly, and adopt proactive security measures to mitigate risks. As cybercriminals continue to exploit new vulnerabilities and tactics, continuous learning and adaptation are essential for maintaining robust security postures.
