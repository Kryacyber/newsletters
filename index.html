Cybersecurity Weekly: AI-Driven Insights on Emerging Threats and Major Disruptions

21 September, 2025

Summary

This week’s cybersecurity landscape saw critical vulnerabilities, high-profile breaches, and emerging threats. Key highlights include a zero-click exploit in OpenAI’s ChatGPT, a critical deserialization flaw in Fortra’s GoAnywhere MFT, and a major token validation failure in Microsoft Entra ID. Meanwhile, law enforcement in the UK arrested members of the Scattered Spider hacking group, and AWS released its Summer 2025 SOC 1 report. Additionally, attackers leveraged ClickFix lures to deliver malware, and LastPass warned of a widespread macOS infostealer campaign. These developments underscore the urgency of proactive security measures and vigilance against evolving threats.

Vulnerabilities

  • Fortra Patches Critical GoAnywhere MFT Vulnerability 2025-09-22T07:50:52.000Z

    Fortra has addressed a critical deserialization flaw in GoAnywhere MFT, allowing attackers to execute command injection. The vulnerability, tracked as CVE-2025-10035, has been assigned a CVSS score of 10.

  • Microsoft Patches Critical Entra ID Flaw Enabling Global Admin Impersonation 2025-09-22T05:47:00.000Z

    Microsoft fixed a critical token validation failure in Entra ID, which could allow attackers to impersonate any user, including Global Administrators. The flaw, CVE-2025-55241, has a CVSS score of 10.0.

Malware

  • DPRK Hackers Use ClickFix to Deliver BeaverTail Malware in Crypto Job Scams 2025-09-21T10:56:00.000Z

    North Korean hackers are using ClickFix-style lures to distribute BeaverTail malware in cryptocurrency job scams. The attackers target marketing and trading roles rather than software development.

  • LastPass Warns of Fake Repositories Infecting macOS with Atomic Infostealer 2025-09-20T07:07:00.000Z

    LastPass has alerted users to a widespread infostealer campaign targeting macOS through fake GitHub repositories distributing Atomic malware.

Data Breaches

  • ShadowLeak Zero-Click Flaw Leaks Gmail Data via OpenAI ChatGPT Deep Research Agent 2025-09-20T05:31:00.000Z

    Researchers discovered a zero-click exploit in OpenAI’s ChatGPT Deep Research agent that allows attackers to leak Gmail inbox data with a single crafted email.

  • SonicWall Urges Password Resets After Cloud Backup Breach Affecting Under 5% of Customers 2025-09-18T14:12:00.000Z

    SonicWall has detected suspicious activity in its cloud backup service, exposing firewall configuration files for less than 5% of its users. Customers are advised to reset credentials.

Policy & Legal

  • U.K. Arrests Two Teen Scattered Spider Hackers Linked to August 2024 TfL Cyber Attack 2025-09-19T07:05:00.000Z

    UK law enforcement arrested two teens from the Scattered Spider hacking group, who allegedly participated in an August 2024 cyber attack on Transport for London (TfL).

General Security News

  • Cyberattack Disrupts Check-In Systems at Major European Airports 2025-09-20T15:33:00.000Z

    A cyberattack disrupted electronic check-in systems at major European airports, forcing passengers to rely on manual check-in and boarding processes.

  • Summer 2025 SOC 1 Report Now Available with 183 Services in Scope 2025-09-19T16:28:41.000Z

    AWS has released its Summer 2025 SOC 1 report, covering 183 services from July 1, 2024, to June 30, 2025, demonstrating continuous compliance with security controls.

  • AWS Organizations Now Supports Full IAM Language for Service Control Policies 2025-09-19T17:50:10.000Z

    AWS Organizations now supports full IAM policy language for service control policies (SCPs), enabling advanced access controls with conditions, resource ARNs, and wildcards.

Conclusion

This week’s cybersecurity news highlights the persistent threat of zero-click exploits, critical vulnerabilities, and coordinated attacks by state-sponsored groups. Organizations must prioritize patch management, monitor emerging threats like ShadowLeak, and strengthen their incident response plans. The arrests of Scattered Spider members and the widespread infostealer campaign underscore the importance of proactive security measures and user awareness.
