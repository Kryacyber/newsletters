Cybersecurity Weekly: AI-Driven Insights on Emerging Threats and Major Disruptions

21 September, 2025

Summary

This week’s top cybersecurity stories highlight critical vulnerabilities, evolving attack tactics, and regulatory updates. A cyberattack disrupted European airport systems, while AWS expanded its security controls and IAM capabilities. Researchers uncovered a zero-click flaw in OpenAI’s ChatGPT, and SonicWall advised credential resets after exposed firewall backups. The U.K. arrested teen hackers linked to a TfL attack, and North Korean-linked actors used ClickFix lures to deploy malware. Meanwhile, LastPass warned of macOS-targeted infostealer campaigns, and Fortra patched a critical GoAnywhere vulnerability. Microsoft disclosed a severe token validation flaw in Entra ID, emphasizing the urgent need for proactive security measures across cloud and endpoint defenses.

Vulnerabilities

  • Fortra Patches Critical GoAnywhere MFT Vulnerability 2025-09-22

    A critical deserialization vulnerability (CVE-2025-10035) in Fortra’s GoAnywhere MFT could allow command injection, posing a severe risk to managed file transfer systems.

  • Microsoft Patches Critical Entra ID Flaw Enabling Global Admin Impersonation 2025-09-22

    Microsoft fixed a critical token validation flaw (CVE-2025-55241) in Entra ID, which could allow attackers to impersonate Global Administrators across any tenant.

Malware

  • DPRK Hackers Use ClickFix to Deliver BeaverTail Malware in Crypto Job Scams 2025-09-21

    North Korean-linked threat actors exploited ClickFix lures to deliver BeaverTail and InvisibleFerret malware in cryptocurrency job scams.

  • LastPass Warns of Fake Repositories Infecting macOS with Atomic Infostealer 2025-09-20

    Fraudulent GitHub repositories distributed Atomic infostealer malware to macOS users, targeting LastPass and other security tools.

Data Breaches

  • ShadowLeak Zero-Click Flaw Leaks Gmail Data via OpenAI ChatGPT Deep Research Agent 2025-09-20

    A zero-click vulnerability in OpenAI’s ChatGPT Deep Research agent allowed attackers to leak Gmail inbox data with a single crafted email.

  • SonicWall Urges Password Resets After Cloud Backup Breach Affecting Under 5% of Customers 2025-09-18

    SonicWall advised credential resets after firewall backup files were exposed, impacting less than 5% of its customer base.

Policy & Legal

  • U.K. Arrests Two Teen Scattered Spider Hackers Linked to August 2024 TfL Cyber Attack 2025-09-19

    Law enforcement arrested two members of the Scattered Spider hacking group for their alleged role in a 2024 TfL cyberattack.

General Security News

  • Cyberattack Disrupts Check-In Systems at Major European Airports 2025-09-20

    A cyberattack forced major European airports to switch to manual check-in and boarding after disrupting electronic systems.

  • Summer 2025 SOC 1 Report Available for 183 AWS Services 2025-09-19

    AWS published its Summer 2025 SOC 1 report, covering 183 services and demonstrating ongoing compliance with security controls.

  • AWS Organizations Now Supports Full IAM Language for Service Control Policies 2025-09-19

    AWS Organizations now supports full IAM language for service control policies, enabling more granular access controls.

Key Takeaways

This week’s news underscores the critical need for proactive security measures, including patch management, credential hygiene, and monitoring for emerging threats. Organizations must prioritize vulnerability remediation, especially for cloud and identity-related flaws, while staying vigilant against sophisticated attack vectors like zero-click exploits and supply chain compromises. The legal actions against teen hackers highlight the growing global focus on cybercrime, while regulatory updates from AWS demonstrate the evolving landscape of compliance and security controls.
