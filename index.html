Cybersecurity Weekly Digest: Critical Vulnerabilities, Global Threats, and Emerging Attack Trends

This week’s cybersecurity landscape highlights critical vulnerabilities, global threats, and emerging attack trends. Key insights include a massive telecom attack thwarted at the UN, a sophisticated fraud ring dismantled in Europe, and a critical patch bypass in Supermicro systems. Microsoft’s Entra ID exposure and GitHub’s npm security overhaul underscore the urgency of proactive measures. Meanwhile, a Chinese-speaking SEO poisoning campaign and a Libraesva ESG flaw reveal persistent state-sponsored and supply chain risks. Stay vigilant—these threats demand immediate attention.

Vulnerabilities

  • 2025-09-23 Massive Telecom Threat Stopped at UN

    A coordinated attack targeting telecom infrastructure was thwarted at the UN headquarters, involving over 300 servers and 100,000 SIM cards designed to mimic cellphones and overwhelm networks.

  • 2025-09-23 Patch Bypassed for Supermicro Vulnerability

    Researchers discovered a method to bypass a patch for a critical Supermicro vulnerability, allowing attackers to exploit a Baseboard Management Controller (BMC) flaw.

  • 2025-09-24 State-Sponsored Hackers Exploiting Libraesva ESG Flaw

    State-sponsored threat actors are exploiting a command injection vulnerability in Libraesva’s Email Security Gateway (ESG), posing a medium-severity risk.

Malware

  • 2025-09-23 BadIIS Malware Spreads via SEO Poisoning

    A Chinese-speaking threat actor is using SEO poisoning to deploy BadIIS malware, targeting East and Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, by redirecting traffic and planting web shells.

Data Breaches

  • 2025-09-23 Jaguar Land Rover Extends Shutdown After Cyberattack

    Jaguar Land Rover has extended its production shutdown until at least October 1st following a cyberattack that disrupted operations.

  • 2025-09-23 Stellantis Discloses Data Breach

    Automotive giant Stellantis disclosed a data breach involving stolen customer contact information from a third-party service provider.

Policy & Legal

  • 2025-09-23 Eurojust Arrests 5 in €100M Cryptocurrency Fraud

    European law enforcement arrested five suspects in a €100 million cryptocurrency investment fraud scheme targeting victims in France, Germany, Italy, and Spain.

General Security News

  • 2025-09-19 AWS Expands IAM Support for Service Control Policies

    AWS Organizations now supports full IAM policy language for service control policies, enabling more granular access control and enhanced security.

  • 2025-09-23 Microsoft Entra ID Silent Compromise Risk

    Researchers found that all Microsoft Entra tenants were exposed to a silent compromise via invisible actor tokens, highlighting risks in identity management.

  • 2025-09-23 GitHub Strengthens npm Supply Chain Security

    GitHub is mandating 2FA and short-lived tokens to address supply chain attacks in the npm ecosystem, including the recent Shai-Hulud attack.

Conclusion

This week’s cybersecurity news underscores the evolving threat landscape, with critical vulnerabilities, state-sponsored attacks, and supply chain risks demanding immediate attention. Organizations must prioritize proactive measures, such as patch management, identity security, and supply chain hardening, to mitigate emerging threats. Stay vigilant and adapt to these challenges to safeguard digital assets.
