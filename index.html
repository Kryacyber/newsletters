Cybersecurity Roundup: Critical Disruptions, Emerging Threats, and Law Enforcement Actions

This week’s cybersecurity roundup highlights critical disruptions in critical infrastructure, emerging threats in cloud and AI security, and ongoing campaigns targeting enterprise and consumer systems. From airport check-in failures to zero-click exploits in ChatGPT, attackers are leveraging sophisticated techniques to compromise sensitive data. Meanwhile, law enforcement takes action against hacking groups, and cloud providers enhance security controls. Stay informed as these trends shape the evolving threat landscape.

General Security News

  • Cyberattack Disrupts Check-In Systems at Major European Airports (2025-09-20)

    A cyberattack disrupted electronic check-in systems at major European airports, forcing passengers to rely on manual check-in and boarding. The incident affected Collins Aerospace systems, impacting passenger check-ins, boarding passes, and luggage handling.

  • Summer 2025 SOC 1 Report Now Available (2025-09-19)

    AWS has released its Summer 2025 SOC 1 report, covering 183 services over a 12-month period. The report demonstrates AWS’s commitment to adhering to security and compliance standards.

  • AWS Organizations Now Supports Full IAM Language for SCPs (2025-09-19)

    AWS Organizations now supports full IAM policy language for Service Control Policies (SCPs), allowing for more granular access control and enhanced security management.

  • Airport Cyberattack Disrupts More Flights Across Europe (2025-09-21)

    An ongoing cyberattack has disrupted airport operations across Europe, affecting passenger check-ins, boarding passes, and luggage handling systems.

Policy & Legal

  • U.K. Arrests Two Teen Scattered Spider Hackers (2025-09-19)

    Law enforcement in the U.K. has arrested two members of the Scattered Spider hacking group, linked to a 2024 cyberattack on Transport for London (TfL).

Data Breaches

  • 600k Hit by Healthcare Breaches, Major ShinyHunters Hacks (2025-09-19)

    Over 600,000 individuals were affected by healthcare breaches, alongside major hacks targeting ShinyHunters, with DeepSeek’s coding bias also drawing attention.

Malware

  • DPRK Hackers Use ClickFix to Deliver BeaverTail Malware (2025-09-18)

    North Korean hackers are using ClickFix-style lures to deliver BeaverTail malware in crypto job scams, targeting marketing and trading roles.

  • LastPass Warns of Fake Repositories Infecting macOS (2025-09-20)

    LastPass has issued a warning about fake GitHub repositories distributing Atomic infostealer malware, targeting macOS users.

Vulnerabilities

  • ShadowLeak Zero-Click Flaw Leaks Gmail Data via ChatGPT (2025-09-20)

    A zero-click vulnerability in OpenAI’s ChatGPT Deep Research agent allows attackers to leak Gmail inbox data with a single crafted email.

  • ChatGPT Targeted in Server-Side Data Theft Attack (2025-09-21)

    ChatGPT has been targeted in a server-side data theft attack, highlighting ongoing risks in AI-driven platforms.

As cyber threats continue to evolve, organizations must prioritize proactive security measures, stay updated on emerging vulnerabilities, and enforce robust access controls. The recent disruptions in critical infrastructure and the growing sophistication of attacks underscore the need for continuous vigilance in cybersecurity.
