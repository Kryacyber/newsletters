AI-Driven Insights on Emerging Threats and Major Disruptions

21 September, 2025

Summary

Welcome to this week’s cybersecurity newsletter, where we highlight critical vulnerabilities, operational disruptions, and law enforcement actions against cybercriminals. From zero-click exploits in AI platforms to high-severity flaws in enterprise software, attackers continue to exploit sophisticated techniques. Meanwhile, cloud providers and tech firms issue urgent security advisories, while law enforcement takes action against threat actors. Stay informed to protect your organization from evolving threats.

Vulnerabilities

  • Fortra Patches Critical GoAnywhere MFT Vulnerability (2025-09-22)

    A critical deserialization flaw in Fortra’s GoAnywhere MFT (CVE-2025-10035) could allow attackers to execute arbitrary commands, posing a severe risk to managed file transfer systems.

  • Microsoft Patches Critical Entra ID Flaw Enabling Global Admin Impersonation (2025-09-22)

    Microsoft addressed a critical token validation failure in Entra ID (CVE-2025-55241), which could allow attackers to impersonate any user, including Global Administrators, across any tenant.

Malware

  • ShadowLeak Zero-Click Flaw Leaks Gmail Data via OpenAI ChatGPT Deep Research Agent (2025-09-20)

    Researchers discovered a zero-click exploit in OpenAI’s ChatGPT Deep Research agent that allows attackers to leak Gmail inbox data without user interaction.

  • DPRK Hackers Use ClickFix to Deliver BeaverTail Malware in Crypto Job Scams (2025-09-21)

    North Korean hackers exploited ClickFix-style lures to deliver BeaverTail malware in cryptocurrency job scams, targeting marketing and trading roles.

  • LastPass Warns of Fake Repositories Infecting macOS with Atomic Infostealer (2025-09-20)

    LastPass alerted users to fake GitHub repositories distributing Atomic infostealer malware, targeting macOS users with password-stealing tools.

Data Breaches

  • SonicWall Urges Password Resets After Cloud Backup Breach (2025-09-18)

    SonicWall advised customers to reset credentials after threat actors accessed cloud backup files for under 5% of its firewall users.

Policy & Legal

  • U.K. Arrests Two Teen Scattered Spider Hackers Linked to August 2024 TfL Cyber Attack (2025-09-19)

    Law enforcement arrested two members of the Scattered Spider hacking group in connection with an August 2024 cyberattack targeting Transport for London.

General Security News

  • Cyberattack Disrupts Check-In Systems at Major European Airports (2025-09-20)

    A cyberattack disrupted electronic check-in systems at major European airports, forcing passengers to rely on manual check-in and boarding procedures.

  • Summer 2025 SOC 1 Report Available for 183 AWS Services (2025-09-19)

    AWS released its Summer 2025 SOC 1 report, covering 183 services and demonstrating continuous compliance with security controls.

  • AWS Organizations Now Supports Full IAM Language for Service Control Policies (2025-09-19)

    AWS Organizations now allows full IAM policy language for service control policies, enabling more granular access control in multi-account environments.

Key Takeaways

This week’s cybersecurity news underscores the persistent threat of zero-click exploits, critical vulnerabilities in enterprise software, and the evolving tactics of state-sponsored attackers. Organizations must prioritize patching, monitor cloud backups, and stay vigilant against fake repositories. Law enforcement actions against hacking groups highlight the importance of proactive threat intelligence. Stay updated to safeguard your systems against emerging risks.
