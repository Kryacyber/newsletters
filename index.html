Krya Solutions: Cybersecurity Weekly

AI-Driven Insights on Emerging Threats and Major Disruptions

21 September, 2025

Weekly Summary

This week, critical vulnerabilities in Microsoft Entra ID and Fortra’s GoAnywhere MFT, alongside a zero-click exploit in OpenAI’s Deep Research agent, highlight the urgent need for proactive security measures. Cyberattacks disrupted European airport operations, while North Korean-linked groups leveraged ClickFix lures to deploy malware. Additionally, exposed SonicWall firewall backups and a macOS infostealer campaign targeting LastPass users underscore the evolving threat landscape. Stay informed and vigilant.

Vulnerabilities

  • Fortra Patches Critical GoAnywhere MFT Vulnerability

    2025-09-22

    A critical deserialization flaw (CVE-2025-10035) in Fortra’s GoAnywhere MFT could enable command injection, posing a severe risk to managed file transfer operations.

  • Microsoft Patches Critical Entra ID Flaw

    2025-09-22

    A token validation failure in Microsoft Entra ID (CVE-2025-55241) allowed attackers to impersonate Global Administrators across tenants, highlighting a critical privilege escalation risk.

Malware

  • DPRK Hackers Use ClickFix Lures to Deliver BeaverTail Malware

    2025-09-21

    North Korean-linked threat actors exploited ClickFix-style lures to deploy BeaverTail malware in cryptocurrency job scams, targeting marketing and trading roles.

  • LastPass Warns of Fake macOS Infostealer Campaign

    2025-09-20

    Fake GitHub repositories distributed Atomic infostealer malware to macOS users, posing a significant risk to password manager users.

Data Breaches

  • ShadowLeak Zero-Click Flaw Leaks Gmail Data via OpenAI

    2025-09-20

    A zero-click vulnerability in OpenAI’s Deep Research agent allowed attackers to leak Gmail inbox data with a single crafted email.

  • SonicWall Urges Password Resets After Cloud Backup Breach

    2025-09-18

    SonicWall disclosed a breach where threat actors accessed firewall backup files, affecting under 5% of customers and prompting password resets.

Policy & Legal

  • U.K. Arrests Two Teen Scattered Spider Hackers

    2025-09-19

    Law enforcement arrested two members of the Scattered Spider hacking group in connection with an August 2024 cyberattack on Transport for London.

General Security News

  • Cyberattack Disrupts Check-In Systems at Major European Airports

    2025-09-20

    A cyberattack forced major European airports to revert to manual check-ins, disrupting travel operations and highlighting vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure.

  • AWS Releases Summer 2025 SOC 1 Report

    2025-09-19

    AWS published its Summer 2025 SOC 1 report, covering 183 services and demonstrating continuous compliance with security controls.

  • AWS Enhances IAM Policy Support for Service Control Policies

    2025-09-19

    AWS Organizations now supports full IAM language for service control policies, enabling granular access control and resource targeting.

Key Takeaways

This week’s headlines underscore the critical need for organizations to prioritize patch management, monitor emerging threats, and enhance endpoint security. The proliferation of zero-click exploits and supply-chain attacks demands proactive measures, while disruptions in critical infrastructure emphasize the need for resilience planning. Security professionals must stay vigilant and adapt to evolving threats.
